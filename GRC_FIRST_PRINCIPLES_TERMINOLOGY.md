# GRC First-Principles Terminology Mapping for Islamic Finance

**Purpose**: Translate technical GRC jargon into plain language that Islamic Finance practitioners (Shariah scholars, compliance officers, finance managers) can understand, while maintaining fidelity to ISO 37301, ISO 31000, COSO, IFSB, and AAOIFI standards.

**Date**: 2025-01-14
**Standards Foundation**: ISO 37301 (CMS) + ISO 31000 (Risk) + COSO (Controls) + IFSB/AAOIFI (Shariah)

---

## 1. PLATFORM IDENTITY & CORE MESSAGING

### Current State Issues:
- "Islamic Finance Workflows" - too generic, misses GRC purpose
- "GRC" acronym - opaque to non-specialists
- "Compliance Management System" - bureaucratic jargon

### **Proposed Platform Name:**

**Option A (Direct):**
**"Islamic Finance Compliance System"**
*Tagline: "Built on ISO 37301 โข Aligned with IFSB & AAOIFI"*

**Option B (Clearer Purpose):**
**"Shariah Compliance Management Platform"**
*Tagline: "Ensure halal operations โข Meet regulatory requirements"*

**Option C (User Benefit First):**
**"Islamic Finance Assurance Platform"**
*Tagline: "Maintain Shariah compliance โข Manage regulatory obligations โข Control operational risks"*

**RECOMMENDATION**: **Option C** - "Assurance" is a COSO term that resonates with boards, and clearly communicates value (not just "compliance checking" but comprehensive assurance).

## 2. CORE GRC CONCEPTS โ PLAIN LANGUAGE

### A. Governance โ "Oversight & Accountability"

| Current GRC Jargon | Standards Basis | Plain Language for Islamic Finance |
|-------------------|----------------|-----------------------------------|
| **Governance** | COSO Component 1 | **"Oversight & Accountability"** |
| **Governance Framework** | ISO 37301 ยง 5 | **"Oversight Structure"** |
| **Board Oversight** | IFSB-10 Part II | **"Board Accountability"** |
| **Governance Model** | IFSB-10 ยง 4.1 | **"How we ensure compliance"** |
| **Three Lines Model** | IIA Framework | **"Three Layers of Checking"** (1st: Management owns, 2nd: Compliance monitors, 3rd: Audit verifies) |

**Why**: "Governance" is abstract. "Oversight & Accountability" immediately clarifies WHO does WHAT and WHO is responsible.
