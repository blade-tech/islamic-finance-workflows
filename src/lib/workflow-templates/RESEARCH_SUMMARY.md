## AAOIFI SS-9: Ijarah and Ijarah Muntahia Bittamleek

### Scope
- Covers Ijarah tul Aayaan (asset leasing)
- Includes simple Ijarah and Ijarah Muntahia Bittamleek (IMB)
- Applies when institution is lessor or lessee

### Key Requirements

#### 1. Asset Ownership (§3/1)
- **Rule**: Institution must own the asset before leasing it
- **Workflow Gate**: Verify legal ownership documentation before Ijarah contract
- **Evidence**: Title deed, purchase invoice, asset registration

#### 2. Delivery Before Rent (§4/4)
- **Rule**: Asset must be delivered to lessee before rental payments begin
- **Workflow Gate**: HARD GATE - Cannot collect rent until delivery confirmed
- **Evidence**: Delivery receipt, handover certificate, lessee acknowledgment

#### 3. Rental Determination
- **Rule**: Rent determined at contract start
- **Flexibility**: Future periods can use clear benchmark/formula
- **Workflow Gate**: Rental schedule approval by both parties

#### 4. Maintenance Responsibility
- **Rule**: Lessor responsible for major maintenance (structural)
- **Flexibility**: Operational maintenance can be allocated to lessee by agreement
- **Workflow Gate**: Maintenance responsibility matrix in contract

#### 5. Takaful Coverage
- **Rule**: Lessor bears risk of asset damage/loss
- **Best Practice**: Lessor should obtain Takaful insurance
- **Workflow Gate**: Takaful policy in place before delivery

#### 6. Permitted Use
- **Rule**: Asset cannot be leased for prohibited purposes
- **Workflow Gate**: Lessee declaration of intended use (must be Shariah-compliant)

#### 7. Sale and Leaseback (for IMB)
- **Rule**: Reasonable time gap required between sale and leaseback
- **Rationale**: Allow for value/condition changes
- **Workflow Gate**: Minimum waiting period verification

### Section Structure (8 sections)
1. Scope of the standard
2. Promise to lease an asset
3. Acquisition of asset or usufruct
4. Concluding Ijarah contract and forms
5. Subject matter of Ijarah
6. Guarantees and treatment of receivables
7. Changes to Ijarah contract
8. Transfer of ownership in IMB

## AAOIFI FAS-28: Murabaha and Other Deferred Payment Sales

### Effective Date
- Approved: September 2017
- Effective: January 1, 2019
- Supersedes: FAS 2 (Murabaha to Purchase Orderer) and FAS 20 (Deferred Payment Sales)

### Scope
- Accounting for Murabaha contracts by Islamic banks/IFIs
- Covers recognition, measurement, disclosure
- Addresses unique Islamic finance requirement: legal asset ownership before resale

### Key Accounting Requirements

#### 1. Cost Disclosure (§3)
- **Rule**: Full transparency on acquisition cost and markup
- **Workflow Gate**: Documented cost breakdown before sale contract
- **Evidence**: Purchase invoice, cost statement, profit margin approval

#### 2. Asset Ownership Before Sale (§4)
- **Rule**: Institution must own asset before selling to customer
- **Workflow Gate**: HARD GATE - Asset must be in institution's inventory
- **Evidence**: Purchase receipt, warehouse entry, inventory record

#### 3. Promise vs Contract (§5)
- **Rule**: Distinguish between binding promise and actual sale contract
- **Workflow Gate**: Separate promise document from Murabaha sale agreement
- **Evidence**: Promise letter (non-binding), sale contract (binding)

#### 4. Inventory Recognition
- **Accounting**: Asset recorded in inventory when owned by institution
- **Workflow Gate**: Accounting entry for inventory acquisition

#### 5. Revenue Recognition
- **Accounting**: Murabaha receivable and deferred profit recorded at sale
- **Workflow Gate**: Profit recognition schedule based on payment terms

#### 6. Derecognition
- **Accounting**: Murabaha receivable derecognized when paid or written off
- **Workflow Gate**: Collection tracking and bad debt provisioning

## AAOIFI FAS-3: Mudaraba Financing

### Status
- Original standard being revised
- Will be combined with FAS 4 (Musharaka) into new "Participatory Ventures" standard
- Current FAS 3 still applicable until new standard issued

### Scope
- Mudaraba where Islamic bank acts as rab al-mal (capital provider)
- Mudaraba where Islamic bank acts as mudarib (entrepreneur/manager)

### Key Requirements

#### 1. Capital Verification (§2)
- **Rule**: Capital (ras al-mal) must be clearly quantified
- **Workflow Gate**: HARD GATE - Capital transfer verification before Mudaraba starts
- **Evidence**: Bank transfer confirmation, capital certificate

#### 2. Profit-Sharing Ratio (§3)
- **Rule**: Profit-sharing percentage determined upfront (not fixed amount)
- **Workflow Gate**: HARD GATE - Profit-sharing ratio in contract (cannot be 100% to one party)
- **Evidence**: Signed agreement with clear ratio (e.g., 60% rab al-mal, 40% mudarib)

#### 3. Loss Allocation (§4)
- **Rule**: Financial losses borne by rab al-mal only (unless mudarib negligent)
- **Workflow Gate**: HARD GATE - Loss provisions cannot be allocated to mudarib
- **Evidence**: Loss policy documentation, negligence investigation if losses occur

#### 4. Mudarib Management
- **Rule**: Mudarib has full authority to manage Mudaraba business
- **Workflow Gate**: Management scope definition (within Shariah parameters)

#### 5. Investment Restrictions
- **Rule**: Mudarib cannot invest in prohibited activities
- **Workflow Gate**: Investment policy and sector restrictions approved

## QCB (Qatar Central Bank) Islamic Banking Requirements

### Capital Adequacy (Effective January 1, 2024)
- **Standard**: Basel III framework and IFSB-23
- **Rule**: Capital Adequacy Ratio (Pillar I) per new QCB Circular No. 33/2022
- **Workflow Impact**: Capital requirement calculations for new products
- **Evidence**: CAR calculations, IFSB-23 compliance report

### Centralized Shariah Supervision
- **Initiative**: QCB moving toward centralized Shariah governance (following Malaysia, Indonesia models)
- **Goal**: Harmonize Shariah-compliant products across Qatar
- **Workflow Impact**: National-level Shariah Board approvals may be required
- **Status**: In development (2024-2030 strategy)

### Strategic Framework (2024-2030)
- **Theme**: Islamic finance as cross-cutting priority
- **Focus Areas**: Innovation, efficiency, investor protection
- **ESG Integration**: Sustainability requirements for Islamic products
- **Workflow Impact**: ESG/VBI modules for future products

### Prudential Requirements
- **Standards**: Basel Committee on Banking Supervision guidelines
- **Adoption**: Qatar early adopter of Basel III
- **Workflow Impact**: Risk management controls (credit risk, operational risk, market risk)

### Retail Lending Guidelines
- **Rule**: QCB directives on retail Islamic financing products
- **Workflow Impact**: Consumer protection requirements, disclosure standards
- **Evidence**: Customer disclosures, terms transparency

## QFCRA (Qatar Financial Centre Regulatory Authority) IBANK Module

### Framework Introduction
- **Year**: 2015
- **Basis**: Basel Committee (BCBS) + IFSB standards
- **Scope**: Islamic banking firms operating in QFC

### Prudential Rules

#### 1. Operational Requirements
- **Rule**: Governance, internal controls, risk management frameworks
- **Workflow Impact**: Operational risk controls in workflows
- **Evidence**: Policies and procedures documentation

#### 2. Risk Management (IBANK Section 1.3.12 for Ijarah)
- **Rule**: Comprehensive risk management for Islamic products
- **Specific**: Ijarah asset risk, ownership risk, Shariah non-compliance risk
- **Workflow Impact**: Risk assessment gates before product launch
- **Evidence**: Risk assessment reports, mitigation plans

#### 3. Shariah Compliance
- **Rule**: Shariah governance framework mandatory
- **Components**: Shariah Board, Shariah Compliance Officer, internal audit
- **Workflow Impact**: Shariah Board approval gates, SCO ex-ante reviews
- **Evidence**: Board resolutions, SCO review reports

#### 4. Credit Risk Framework (2024-2025 Updates)
- **Standard**: IFSB approach for credit risk
- **Rule**: Credit risk management, problem asset classification, provisioning
- **Workflow Impact**: Credit approval gates, provisioning workflows
- **Evidence**: Credit memos, risk ratings, provision calculations

#### 5. Large Exposures Framework
- **Rule**: Concentration limits on financing exposures
- **Workflow Impact**: Exposure monitoring workflows
- **Evidence**: Exposure reports, limit compliance documentation

#### 6. Market Risk (2025 Amendments)
- **Proposal**: Revised market risk calculation approach
- **Status**: Consultation phase (2025)
- **Workflow Impact**: Market risk controls for investment products (Mudaraba)
- **Evidence**: Value-at-Risk calculations, stress testing

#### 7. Equity Investment in Funds
- **Rule**: Prudential treatment for equity investments
- **Workflow Impact**: Investment approval gates for funds
- **Evidence**: Investment proposals, due diligence reports

#### 8. Breach Notification (Rule 2.1.8)
- **Rule**: Notify QFCRA of prudential breaches immediately
- **Workflow Impact**: Breach detection and reporting workflows
- **Evidence**: Breach notifications, remediation plans

### 2024 Updates
- **Focus**: IFSB credit risk framework implementation
- **Timeline**: July 1, 2024 effective date (proposed)
- **Impact**: Enhanced credit risk management requirements
