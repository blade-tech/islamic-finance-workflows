#### 1. Qatar Has Dual Regulatory Structure âš ï¸
**Finding**: Two distinct regulators with different approaches
- **QCB**: Prescriptive, detailed, mandatory AAOIFI
- **QFCRA**: Principles-based, selective AAOIFI reference
- **Implication**: GRC system must support multi-regulator jurisdictions

- **Implication**: Qatar SSB governance is entity-centric, not centrally directed

#### 3. Comprehensive AAOIFI Adoption at QCB âœ…

- QFCRA uses selective reference
- **Implication**: Different accounting obligations based on regulator

## Shariah Governance Models

### QFCRA SSB Model

**Structure**:
- Minimum 3 members
- No maximum specified
- No Central Shariah Board

**Independence**:
- Conflict of interest rules
- No specific position limit stated
- Term not specified

**Decision-Making**:
- Quorum not specified
- Voting procedure not detailed
- Dissent recording recommended

**Reporting**:
- Annual SSB report to QFCRA (within 3 months of year-end)
- Quarterly to Board
- Public disclosure in annual report

**Internal Function**:
- Shariah compliance function recommended
- No mandatory Shariah audit

### QCB SSB Model

**Structure**:
- Minimum 3 members
- No maximum specified
- **No Central Shariah Board** (decentralized model)

**Independence**:
- Strict conflict of interest rules
- Maximum 3 SSB positions per scholar
- Cooling-off period: 2 years

**Decision-Making**:
- Quorum: Majority of members
- Voting: Majority decision
- Dissent must be recorded in minutes
- Permanent retention of all SSB decisions

**Reporting**:
- Quarterly SSB activity reports to QCB
- Annual SSB opinion to QCB and public
- Monthly internal reports to Board
- Event-driven SNCR reporting

**Internal Function**:
- **Mandatory** Internal Shariah Audit function
- Independent reporting to SSB
- Annual audit plan required
- QCB oversight

**Key Difference**: QCB requires **mandatory Internal Shariah Audit**; QFCRA does not.

## AAOIFI Adoption Comparison

### QFCRA: Selective Reference

**Explicitly Mandatory**:
- GSIFI No. 1, 2, 3 (Governance standards for IFIs)
- FAS 12 (General Presentation - Interim)
- FAS 13 (General Presentation - Disclosure)
- FAS 18 (Foreign Currency Transactions)

**Implicitly Required** (via industry practice):
- ~20 Shariah Standards (SS) for product compliance
- Select governance standards (GS)

**Integration Approach**:
- QFCRA rules are primary
- AAOIFI supplements where QFCRA is silent
- IFRS as fallback for gaps

### QCB: Comprehensive Mandatory

**Fully Mandatory**:
- **All AAOIFI Financial Accounting Standards (FAS)** for Islamic banks
- AAOIFI primary, IFRS only as fallback for gaps
- Specific mandatory standards: FAS 25 (Investment in Sukuk), FAS 17 (Investment Accounts)

**Shariah Standards**:
- Not explicitly mandated by QCB
- Entity SSBs determine which SS apply based on products

**Governance Standards**:
- QCB rules incorporate GS principles
- Not verbatim adoption

**Integration Approach**:
- **AAOIFI FAS is the primary framework**
- IFRS applied ONLY where AAOIFI is silent
- **Hierarchy**: AAOIFI > Qatar Law > IFRS

**Key Difference**: QCB mandates AAOIFI FAS comprehensively; QFCRA selectively references standards.

## Evidence & Documentation Standards

### QFCRA

**Retention Period**: 6 years (standard)

**Key Requirements**:
- SSB decisions and minutes: 6 years
- Transaction documentation: 6 years
- Customer records: 6 years
- Format: Physical or electronic (digital preferred)
- Language: English

**Inspection Readiness**:
- Must produce documents within 5 business days for QFCRA
- Organized and indexed

### QCB

**Retention Period**: 10 years (standard), Permanent (SSB fatwas)

**Key Requirements**:
- **SSB fatwas and decisions: PERMANENT retention**
- Financial records: 10 years
- Transaction documentation: 10 years
- Customer records: 10 years from account closure
- Format: Physical AND electronic (both required for critical documents)
- Language: **Arabic primary, English translation required**

**Inspection Readiness**:
- Must produce documents within 3 business days for QCB
- Organized by category with Arabic indexing

**Key Difference**: QCB requires **10 years vs. 6 years**, **bilingual documentation**, and **permanent SSB fatwa retention**.

## Implications for GRC Infrastructure

### System Must Support:

1. **Dual Regulatory Frameworks**
   - User selects: "QCB" or "QFCRA" or "Both"
   - Different obligations activate based on selection
   - Different evidence requirements per regulator

2. **Different AAOIFI Compliance Levels**
   - QCB: Full mandatory FAS
   - QFCRA: Selective reference
   - System must map which standards apply to which regulator

3. **Bilingual Documentation (for QCB)**
   - Arabic primary language
   - English translation required
   - System must support both languages

4. **Variable Retention Periods**
   - QCB: 10 years standard, permanent for SSB
   - QFCRA: 6 years standard
   - System must enforce different retention per regulator

5. **Different Reporting Calendars**
   - QCB: Monthly + Quarterly + Annual cycles
   - QFCRA: Quarterly + Annual cycles
   - System must schedule reports per regulator

6. **Internal Shariah Audit**
   - QCB: Mandatory function required
   - QFCRA: Optional/recommended
   - System must enable Shariah audit workflows for QCB
