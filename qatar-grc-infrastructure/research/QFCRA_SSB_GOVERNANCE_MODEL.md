# QFCRA Shariah Supervision Board Governance Model

## Executive Summary

This document details the complete Shariah governance requirements for Islamic Financial Institutions (IFIs) operating in the Qatar Financial Centre under QFCRA regulation. The model is based on mandatory requirements from ISFI, IBANK, CTRL rules and aligned with AAOIFI Governance Standards.

**Document Version**: 1.0
**Last Updated**: November 2024
**Primary Sources**:
- Islamic Finance Rules 2005 (ISFI) Version 9
- Islamic Banking Business Prudential Rules 2015 (IBANK) Version 11
- Governance and Controlled Functions Rules 2020 (CTRL) Version 7
- AAOIFI Governance Standards (GSIFI No. 1, 2, 3)

## SSB Requirement

### Is SSB Mandatory for QFC Islamic Finance Entities?

**YES** - SSB is mandatory for all Islamic Financial Institutions in QFC

**Source**: ISFI Rule 6.1.1 states unequivocally:
> "An Islamic financial institution must (a) appoint a Shari'a supervisory board"

This requirement applies to:
- Islamic banks (under IBANK rules)
- Islamic investment firms
- Islamic insurance companies (Takaful)
- Firms operating Islamic windows (for the Islamic portion of business)

### Minimum/Maximum Size

**Minimum Size**: 3 members
- **Source**: ISFI Rule 6.1.1(b)(i)
- **Quote**: "ensure that its Shari'a supervisory board consists of at least 3 members"

**Maximum Size**: Not specified
- QFCRA does not impose a maximum limit
- Best practice suggests odd numbers (3, 5, 7) for voting purposes

### Qualifications Required

**Mandatory Qualifications** (ISFI Rule 6.1.1(b)(ii)):
- Members must be "competent to perform their functions as Shari'a supervisory board members"
- Competence assessed based on:
  - Qualifications (Shariah education/certification)
  - Previous experience in Islamic finance
  - Track record on other SSBs

**Documented Assessment Required** (ISFI Rule 6.1.4):
- Factors taken into account for competence assessment
- Qualifications and experience details
- Basis for suitability determination
- Details of other SSB memberships (current and past)

**Typical Qualifications Expected** (Industry Practice):
- Advanced degree in Shariah/Islamic jurisprudence
- Specialized training in Islamic finance
- Understanding of modern financial practices
- Experience in fatwa issuance
- Knowledge of AAOIFI standards

## SSB Composition

### Required Elements

1. **Minimum Three Members** (ISFI 6.1.1(b)(i))
   - All must meet competency requirements
   - Mix of Shariah scholars and experts recommended

2. **Independence Requirements** (ISFI 6.1.1(b)(iv) & 6.1.5)
   - **Prohibited Roles**:
     - Cannot be a director of the institution
     - Cannot be a controller of the institution
     - Must be independent of management
   - **Conflict of Interest Management**:
     - Must not be subject to any conflict of interest
     - Institution must take reasonable steps to ensure independence

3. **Appointment Process** (ISFI 6.1.1(b)(iii))
   - Appointments must be approved by the governing body (Board of Directors)
   - Dismissals require board approval
   - Changes require board approval

### Diversity Considerations

While not explicitly mandated, best practices suggest:
- Mix of senior scholars and emerging experts
- Geographic diversity for international perspectives
- Specialization diversity (fiqh muamalat, accounting, banking)
- Gender diversity where culturally appropriate

## SSB Independence

### Structural Independence Requirements

1. **Governance Separation** (ISFI 6.1.1(b)(iv))
   - SSB members cannot be directors
   - SSB members cannot be controllers
   - SSB operates independently from management

2. **Conflict of Interest Prevention** (ISFI 6.1.5)
   - Institution must ensure no conflicts exist
   - Regular conflict assessments required
   - Disclosure of all potential conflicts

3. **Information Access Rights** (ISFI 6.1.7)
   - Direct access to all relevant records
   - Right to request any information
   - No interference from management
   - Protection from misleading information

### Operational Independence Safeguards

1. **Direct Reporting Lines**
   - Reports directly to Board of Directors
   - Annual report submitted to QFCRA
   - Public disclosure of opinions

2. **Protected Decision-Making**
   - Fatwas and rulings are binding
   - Management cannot override SSB decisions
   - Disputes escalated to board level

3. **Resource Independence**
   - Adequate resources for functioning
   - Independent budget allocation
   - Access to external experts if needed

## SSB Decision-Making Process

### Meeting Requirements

**Frequency**: Not explicitly specified in QFCRA rules
- Best practice: Quarterly minimum
- Additional meetings as needed
- Emergency meetings for urgent matters

### Quorum Requirements

**Not explicitly specified** in QFCRA rules
- Industry practice: Majority (2 of 3 minimum)
- Some institutions require full attendance for major decisions

### Voting Mechanism

**Not explicitly specified** in QFCRA rules
- Common practice:
  - Simple majority for routine matters
  - Unanimous or super-majority for product approvals
  - Dissenting opinions recorded

### Decision Documentation

**Required Documentation** (ISFI 6.1.3):
- All decisions must be recorded
- Fatwas and rulings documented
- Guidelines properly maintained
- Records retained for minimum 6 years

### Decision Types

1. **Product Approvals**
   - New product Shariah compliance
   - Modifications to existing products
   - Contract template approvals

2. **Operational Rulings**
   - Transaction-specific decisions
   - Income purification determinations
   - SNCR remediation approvals

3. **Policy Decisions**
   - Shariah compliance policies
   - Internal review procedures
   - Training requirements

## SSB Reporting

### To QFCRA

**Annual Report Requirement** (ISFI 6.2.2):
- **Deadline**: Within 3 months after financial year end
- **Content**: Must comply with AAOIFI GSIFI No. 1
- **Format**: Written report with SSB signatures

**Required Content** (per AAOIFI GSIFI No. 1):
- SSB opinion on Shariah compliance
- Scope of review conducted
- Procedures performed
- Findings and observations
- Recommendations for improvement
- Statement of management responsibility

**Ad-Hoc Reporting**:
- Material SNCR events
- Significant compliance breaches
- Upon QFCRA request (ISFI 6.1.6)

### To Board of Directors

**Regular Updates**:
- Quarterly reports recommended
- Annual comprehensive report
- Immediate escalation of critical issues

**Report Content**:
- Compliance status overview
- Key decisions made
- Issues requiring board attention
- Risk assessment
- Recommendations

### To Public/Stakeholders

**Financial Statement Disclosures** (IBANK 11.1.9):
- Role and authority of SSB
- SSB members' names
- Basis of Shariah compliance

**Marketing Communications** (ISFI 6.2.3):
- SSB review confirmation
- Product approval status

**Annual Report**:
- SSB report often included
- Demonstrates transparency
- Builds stakeholder confidence

## Internal Shariah Compliance Function

### Requirement for Internal Function

**Mandatory Internal Shariah Review** (ISFI 6.3.1):
- Must perform internal Shariah reviews
- Assess compliance with SSB fatwas and guidelines
- Frequency determined by SSB

### Organizational Structure

**Placement Options** (ISFI 6.3.4):
- Within internal audit function, OR
- Within compliance oversight function
- Must be sufficiently independent

**Typical Structure**:
```
Board of Directors
    ├── Shariah Supervisory Board
    └── Management
         ├── Internal Audit
         │   └── Shariah Audit Unit
         └── Compliance
             └── Shariah Compliance Unit
```

### Roles and Responsibilities

**Shariah Compliance Officer/Department**:
1. **Pre-Execution Review**:
   - Product documentation review
   - Contract verification
   - Process compliance check

2. **Ongoing Monitoring**:
   - Transaction surveillance
   - Income stream verification
   - Operational compliance

3. **Post-Execution Review**:
   - Compliance testing
   - Exception identification
   - Remediation coordination

4. **Reporting**:
   - Regular reports to SSB
   - Management updates
   - SNCR incident reports

### Competency Requirements

**Required Competencies** (ISFI 6.3.4(b)):
- Competent in Shariah matters
- Understanding of Islamic finance
- Knowledge of AAOIFI standards
- Audit/compliance skills

**Recommended Qualifications**:
- Certification in Islamic finance
- Shariah education background
- Professional audit qualification
- Banking operations experience

## Comparison to AAOIFI GS-1 and GS-18

### Alignment with AAOIFI GS-1 (Shariah Supervisory Board)

| Requirement | AAOIFI GS-1 | QFCRA (ISFI) | Alignment |
|------------|------------|--------------|-----------|
| **Minimum Members** | 3 members | 3 members | ✅ Full Alignment |
| **Appointment** | By shareholders | By governing body | ✅ Full Alignment |
| **Independence** | Independent of management | Cannot be directors/controllers | ✅ Full Alignment |
| **Competence** | Shariah knowledge required | Competence assessment required | ✅ Full Alignment |
| **Annual Report** | Required | Required within 3 months | ✅ Full Alignment |
| **Binding Decisions** | Fatwas are binding | SSB decisions binding | ✅ Full Alignment |
| **Meeting Frequency** | Regular meetings | Not specified | ⚠️ Partial - QFCRA less prescriptive |
| **Quorum** | Majority specified | Not specified | ⚠️ Partial - QFCRA less prescriptive |
| **Terms of Reference** | Required | Policy documentation required | ✅ Full Alignment |

### Alignment with AAOIFI GS-18 (Shariah Compliance Function)

| Requirement | AAOIFI GS-18 | QFCRA (ISFI) | Alignment |
|------------|--------------|--------------|-----------|
| **Internal Function** | Mandatory | Mandatory (6.3.1) | ✅ Full Alignment |
| **Independence** | Required | Required (6.3.4) | ✅ Full Alignment |
| **Reporting Lines** | To SSB | Implied through review | ✅ Full Alignment |
| **Competency** | Specified requirements | Competency required | ✅ Full Alignment |
| **Scope of Work** | Comprehensive | Per GSIFI No. 3 | ✅ Full Alignment |

### Key Differences from AAOIFI

1. **Less Prescriptive on Procedures**:
   - QFCRA allows flexibility in meeting frequency
   - No specified quorum requirements
   - Voting procedures not detailed

2. **Integration with Existing Functions**:
   - QFCRA allows Shariah compliance within audit or compliance
   - AAOIFI prefers dedicated function

3. **Documentation Requirements**:
   - QFCRA emphasizes 6-year record retention
   - More specific on assessment documentation

## Best Practices and Recommendations

### SSB Composition
1. **Optimal Size**: 5 members provides good balance
2. **Mix**: 3 Shariah scholars + 2 subject matter experts
3. **Tenure**: Staggered terms to ensure continuity
4. **Succession**: Plan for orderly transitions

### Independence Enhancement
1. **Fixed Terms**: 3-year renewable terms
2. **Cooling-Off**: Period between service and employment
3. **Cross-Directorships**: Limit other SSB memberships
4. **Direct Access**: Regular sessions without management

### Decision Quality
1. **Pre-Meeting Materials**: Comprehensive briefing packs
2. **Expert Input**: Access to technical specialists
3. **Documentation**: Detailed minutes and rationales
4. **Follow-Up**: Track implementation of decisions

### Internal Function Excellence
1. **Career Path**: Develop Islamic finance professionals
2. **Training**: Continuous education program
3. **Technology**: Automated compliance monitoring
4. **Collaboration**: Regular interaction with SSB

## Common Challenges and Solutions

### Challenge 1: SSB Member Availability
- **Solution**: Establish clear time commitments upfront
- Use technology for remote participation
- Schedule meetings well in advance

### Challenge 2: Independence Concerns
- **Solution**: Robust conflict of interest process
- Regular independence assessments
- Clear escalation procedures

### Challenge 3: Technical Complexity
- **Solution**: Provide technical training to SSB
- Use simplified executive summaries
- Engage subject matter experts

### Challenge 4: Implementation Gaps
- **Solution**: Strong internal Shariah function
- Regular compliance monitoring
- Clear accountability framework
