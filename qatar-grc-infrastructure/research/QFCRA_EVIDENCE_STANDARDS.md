# QFCRA Evidence & Documentation Standards

## Executive Summary

This document outlines the comprehensive evidence and documentation requirements for Islamic Financial Institutions operating under QFCRA jurisdiction. It covers what must be documented, how long records must be retained, acceptable formats, and specific requirements for regulatory inspections and audits.

**Document Version**: 1.0
**Last Updated**: November 2024
**Primary Sources**:
- Islamic Finance Rules 2005 (ISFI) Version 9
- Islamic Banking Business Prudential Rules 2015 (IBANK) Version 11
- Governance and Controlled Functions Rules 2020 (CTRL) Version 7
- General Rules 2005 (GENE)

## Evidence Requirements by Obligation Type

### 1. Product Approval Evidence

#### Required Documentation for Each Product Approval

**SSB Product Approval Package**:
1. **Product Proposal Documentation**
   - Product structure and mechanism
   - Underlying Shariah contracts used
   - Process flow and transaction sequence
   - Risk allocation matrix
   - Pricing methodology
   - **Retention**: Lifetime of product + 6 years

2. **Shariah Analysis**
   - Shariah compliance assessment
   - Comparison with AAOIFI standards
   - Identification of Shariah risks
   - Mitigation measures proposed
   - **Retention**: Lifetime of product + 6 years

3. **SSB Resolution**
   - Formal SSB approval/fatwa
   - Conditions and restrictions
   - Implementation guidelines
   - Dissenting opinions (if any)
   - **Retention**: Lifetime of product + 6 years
   - **Source**: ISFI Rule 6.1.3

4. **Legal Documentation**
   - Master agreements
   - Terms and conditions
   - Customer disclosure templates
   - Marketing material samples
   - **Retention**: Lifetime of product + 6 years

5. **Implementation Evidence**
   - System configuration documents
   - Process maps
   - Training materials
   - Control procedures
   - **Retention**: Current version + previous 2 versions

#### Format Requirements
- **Original**: SSB resolutions must be signed originals
- **Electronic**: Scanned copies acceptable for backup
- **Language**: Arabic and English versions where applicable

### 2. Transaction Evidence

#### Required Documentation for Each Transaction

**Individual Transaction Records**:

1. **Contract Documentation**
   - Executed agreements (all parties)
   - Supporting schedules and appendices
   - Amendment documentation
   - **Retention**: 6 years from transaction completion
   - **Source**: General commercial law requirements

2. **Shariah Compliance Evidence**
   - Pre-execution compliance checklist
   - Shariah audit trail
   - Income classification (halal/non-halal)
   - **Retention**: 6 years from transaction completion

3. **Customer Consent**
   - Signed acknowledgment of Islamic nature
   - Profit-sharing ratio agreements (for Mudarabah/Musharakah)
   - Wakalah fee agreements
   - **Retention**: 6 years from relationship end

4. **Financial Records**
   - Payment confirmations
   - Profit calculations
   - Distribution records
   - Zakat calculations (if applicable)
   - **Retention**: 6 years minimum
   - **Source**: IBANK Rule 11.1.9

5. **Operational Documentation**
   - Trade confirmations
   - Settlement instructions
   - Custody records
   - **Retention**: 6 years from transaction

#### Specific Requirements by Product Type

**Murabaha Transactions**:
- Purchase order from customer
- Evidence of asset purchase by bank
- Invoice/receipt from supplier
- Transfer of ownership documentation
- Sale agreement with customer
- Disclosure of cost and profit margin

**Ijarah Transactions**:
- Asset purchase documentation
- Ownership evidence
- Lease agreement
- Maintenance records
- Insurance documentation
- End-of-lease transfer (if applicable)

**Sukuk Transactions**:
- Sukuk prospectus/information memorandum
- Trust deed/declaration
- Asset transfer documentation
- Periodic distribution calculations
- Trustee reports

**Takaful Transactions**:
- Takaful certificate
- Contribution records
- Claims documentation
- Surplus/deficit calculations
- Qard documentation (if applicable)

### 3. SSB Decision Evidence

#### Required Documentation for SSB Decisions

1. **Meeting Documentation**
   - Meeting agenda
   - Briefing materials provided
   - Attendance records
   - **Retention**: 6 years minimum
   - **Source**: ISFI Rule 6.1.3

2. **Decision Records**
   - Detailed minutes of discussions
   - Resolutions passed
   - Voting records
   - Dissenting opinions with rationale
   - **Retention**: 6 years from decision date

3. **Fatwa Documentation**
   - Complete fatwa text
   - Shariah basis and evidence
   - Conditions and limitations
   - Implementation timeline
   - **Format**: Signed original + electronic copies
   - **Retention**: Permanent (while product/service exists)

4. **Follow-Up Actions**
   - Implementation status reports
   - Compliance confirmations
   - Exception reports
   - Corrective actions taken
   - **Retention**: 6 years from action completion

---

### 4. SNCR Event Evidence

#### Required Documentation for SNCR Incidents

1. **Incident Identification**
   - Discovery documentation
   - Initial assessment report
   - Root cause analysis
   - Impact quantification
   - **Timing**: Within 24 hours of discovery
   - **Retention**: 6 years from incident closure

2. **SSB Review and Decision**
   - Incident report to SSB
   - SSB assessment and ruling
   - Remediation requirements
   - Purification determination
   - **Timing**: Next SSB meeting or emergency session
   - **Retention**: 6 years from decision

3. **Remediation Evidence**
   - Corrective action plan
   - Implementation timeline
   - Progress reports
   - Completion confirmation
   - **Retention**: 6 years from completion

4. **Purification Documentation**
   - Non-compliant income calculation
   - Purification amount determination
   - Charity recipient selection
   - Transfer confirmation
   - Charity receipt/acknowledgment
   - **Retention**: 6 years from purification
   - **Audit Trail**: Complete and unbroken

5. **Regulatory Reporting** (if required)
   - SNCR report to QFCRA
   - Supporting documentation
   - Correspondence with regulator
   - **Retention**: 6 years minimum

## Retention Requirements

### Mandatory Retention Periods

| Document Type | Retention Period | Source |
|--------------|------------------|--------|
| **SSB Member Records** | 6 years from departure | ISFI Rule 6.1.3 |
| **SSB Decisions/Fatwas** | Product lifetime + 6 years | ISFI Rule 6.1.3 |
| **Transaction Records** | 6 years from completion | General requirement |
| **Customer Records** | 6 years from relationship end | AML/KYC requirements |
| **Internal Audit Reports** | 6 years from report date | CTRL requirements |
| **Financial Statements** | 10 years | GENE requirements |
| **Operational Loss Data** | 10 years | IBANK Rule 7.3.1 |
| **Regulatory Correspondence** | 6 years minimum | Best practice |

### Special Considerations

1. **Legal Proceedings**: Extend retention if litigation pending
2. **Regulatory Investigation**: Preserve all related records
3. **Product Discontinuation**: Maintain records for 6 years after last transaction
4. **System Migration**: Ensure accessibility of historical records

## Format Requirements

### Acceptable Formats

#### Physical Documents
- **Original Signed Documents**: Required for:
  - SSB resolutions and fatwas
  - Major contracts
  - Regulatory submissions
  - Board approvals

- **Storage Requirements**:
  - Fireproof storage for critical documents
  - Access controlled environment
  - Climate controlled for long-term preservation
  - Off-site backup copies

#### Electronic Documents
- **Acceptable Formats**:
  - PDF/A for long-term archival
  - Signed PDFs with digital certificates
  - Database records with audit trails
  - Scanned images (minimum 300 DPI)

- **Requirements**:
  - Tamper-proof storage
  - Version control
  - Access logging
  - Regular backup
  - Encryption for sensitive data

#### Blockchain/Digital Evidence
- **Current Status**: Not explicitly addressed in QFCRA rules
- **Recommended Approach**:
  - Maintain traditional documentation parallel to blockchain
  - Ensure blockchain records are auditable
  - Provide regulator access to verification tools
  - Document hash values and timestamps

### Language Requirements

1. **Primary Language**: English or Arabic acceptable
2. **Translations**:
   - SSB documents often in Arabic (translation required)
   - Customer documents in language of choice
   - Regulatory submissions in English preferred
3. **Certification**: Official translations for legal documents

## Inspection & Audit Requirements

### What Must Be Available for QFCRA Inspections

#### Immediate Access Required (Within 1 Business Day)

1. **Governance Documents**
   - SSB member appointments and assessments
   - SSB charter and policies
   - Board resolutions on Islamic finance
   - Organizational structure

2. **Compliance Records**
   - Current Shariah compliance status
   - Recent internal Shariah review reports
   - SNCR incidents (last 12 months)
   - Remediation actions in progress

3. **Product Documentation**
   - Approved product list with SSB fatwas
   - Product manuals and procedures
   - Standard contracts and agreements
   - Current marketing materials

4. **Financial Information**
   - Latest financial statements
   - Zakat calculations
   - Profit distribution records
   - Purification records

#### Standard Access (Within 5 Business Days)

1. **Historical Records**
   - Transaction records (per QFCRA request)
   - Customer files (specific accounts)
   - Previous audit reports
   - Historical SSB decisions

2. **Operational Data**
   - System documentation
   - Process maps
   - Training records
   - Incident logs

3. **Risk Information**
   - Risk assessments
   - Control testing results
   - Loss data
   - Key risk indicators

### Inspection Preparation Best Practices

1. **Data Room Preparation**
   - Physical: Dedicated secure room
   - Virtual: Encrypted data room platform
   - Index of available documents
   - Point of contact designated

2. **Document Organization**
   - Logical folder structure
   - Clear naming conventions
   - Version control evident
   - Cross-referencing available

3. **Personnel Availability**
   - SSB members (or representatives)
   - Head of Shariah Compliance
   - Internal Shariah Auditor
   - Senior Management

## Evidence Quality Standards

### Characteristics of Acceptable Evidence

1. **Completeness**
   - All required fields populated
   - Supporting documents attached
   - Approval signatures present
   - Dates clearly indicated

2. **Authenticity**
   - Original or certified copies
   - Proper authorization evident
   - Audit trail maintained
   - No unauthorized alterations

3. **Reliability**
   - Source verification possible
   - Consistent with other records
   - Contemporaneous creation
   - Independent corroboration available

4. **Accessibility**
   - Retrievable within required timeframe
   - Readable format
   - Indexed and searchable
   - Backup copies available

### Common Deficiencies to Avoid

1. **Documentation Gaps**
   - Missing signatures
   - Incomplete forms
   - Missing attachments
   - Broken audit trails

2. **Retention Failures**
   - Premature destruction
   - Lost documents
   - Inaccessible archives
   - System migration losses

3. **Quality Issues**
   - Illegible copies
   - Corrupted electronic files
   - Missing metadata
   - Incorrect versions
