### Category: Shariah Governance

#### UNIFIED-OBL-001: Establish Shariah Supervisory Board (SSB)

**Applicability**: [BOTH]

**Requirement**:
Establish and maintain a Shariah Supervisory Board with minimum 3 qualified Shariah scholars to oversee all Islamic operations.

**QCB Requirement**:
- Source: QCB Law 13/2012, Article 109
- Requirement: Minimum 3 qualified Shariah scholars, maximum 3 SSB positions per scholar
- Frequency: One-time establishment, ongoing maintenance
- Evidence: SSB appointment letters, CVs, Board resolutions
- Timeline: Before commencing Islamic operations

**QFCRA Requirement**:
- Source: ISFI Rule 6.1.1
- Requirement: Minimum 3 members, competence assessment required
- Frequency: Ongoing
- Evidence: Documentation of appointments, competence records
- Timeline: Before conducting Islamic financial business

**Conflict Analysis**:
QCB limits scholars to max 3 SSB positions while QFCRA has no specific limit. For entities under both, apply QCB's stricter limit.

**Unified Implementation Approach**:
1. Appoint minimum 3 scholars meeting both regulators' qualification criteria
2. Ensure scholars hold maximum 3 SSB positions (QCB limit)
3. Document competence assessment (QFCRA requirement)
4. Obtain board resolution and appointment letters

**Related AAOIFI/IFSB Standards**: AAOIFI GS-1, IFSB-10 ยง4.1
**Related Controls**: SG-01, SG-04
**Priority**: Critical

#### UNIFIED-OBL-002: SSB Independence Requirements

**Applicability**: [BOTH]

**Requirement**:
Ensure SSB members maintain independence from management and are free from conflicts of interest.

**QCB Requirement**:
- Source: QCB Circular 68/2015
- Requirement: Strict independence criteria, 2-year cooling-off period
- Frequency: Ongoing
- Evidence: Independence declarations, conflict of interest policies
- Timeline: Continuous monitoring

**QFCRA Requirement**:
- Source: ISFI Rule 6.1.1(b)(iv) and 6.1.5
- Requirement: No SSB member can be director/controller, principles-based approach
- Frequency: Ongoing
- Evidence: Conflict of interest declarations, independence assessments
- Timeline: At appointment and ongoing

**Conflict Analysis**:
QCB has more prescriptive independence criteria including cooling-off period. Apply QCB's stricter requirements when both apply.

**Unified Implementation Approach**:
1. Implement QCB's strict independence criteria (covers QFCRA requirements)
2. Enforce 2-year cooling-off period
3. Annual independence declarations from all SSB members
4. Quarterly conflict of interest reviews

**Related AAOIFI/IFSB Standards**: AAOIFI GS-1, IFSB-10
**Related Controls**: SG-04
**Priority**: Critical

## Regulatory Selection Guide

### Choose QCB if:
- Operating in mainland Qatar
- Retail banking focus
- Need full banking license
- Can support extensive compliance

### Choose QFCRA if:
- Operating in QFC
- International/wholesale focus
- Innovation priority
- English-law preference

### For Groups with Both:
- Apply strictest requirement when conflicting
- Centralize common functions
- Maintain separate reporting
- Coordinate compliance efforts
